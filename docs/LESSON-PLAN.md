# üìÖ 75-Minute Lesson Plan: Multi-Step Equations
**Florida Standard MA.8.AR.2.1 | Grade 7 Pre-Algebra**

## üéØ Lesson Overview

**Topic**: Solving Multi-Step Equations with Variables on Both Sides
**Duration**: 75 minutes
**Grade Level**: 7th Grade
**Platform**: Equation Quest Interactive Game

## üìã Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Solve two-step equations independently (80% accuracy)
2. Apply the distributive property to simplify equations
3. Solve equations with variables on both sides
4. Identify and explain special solutions (infinite/none)
5. Demonstrate problem-solving strategies using multiple approaches

## üìö Materials Needed

- Computer/laptop/tablet for each student (or pairs)
- Equation Quest platform (index.html)
- Projector/screen for demonstration
- Student notebooks for work
- Optional: Gemini AI access for differentiation

## ‚è∞ Lesson Timeline (75 Minutes)

### üî• **Part 1: Hook & Warm-Up** (10 minutes)

**Objective**: Activate prior knowledge and introduce the game

**Activities**:
1. **Game Introduction** (3 min)
   - Open Equation Quest on projector
   - Show the story mode interface
   - Explain the quest: "You're math heroes saving the Kingdom of Algebra!"
   - Demonstrate XP, coins, and streak system

2. **Quick Video Review** (7 min)
   - Click "Video Tutorials"
   - Play "Introduction to Multi-Step Equations" (first 5-7 minutes)
   - Pause at key points to ask:
     - "What operation should we do first?"
     - "Why do we need to do the same thing to both sides?"

**Teacher Notes**:
- Build excitement about the game elements
- Connect to video games students might know
- Emphasize: "You'll learn by playing AND getting smarter!"

---

### üìñ **Part 2: Direct Instruction** (15 minutes)

**Objective**: Model problem-solving and introduce learning progression

**Activities**:

1. **Live Gameplay Demo** (8 min)
   - Start Story Mode Level 1
   - Solve first equation together:
     - Read equation aloud
     - Show 3D visualization
     - Think aloud: "What do I see? What should I do first?"
     - Write steps on board
     - Enter answer in game
   - Celebrate the correct answer and XP earned!
   - Do 1-2 more problems together

2. **3D Balance Explanation** (3 min)
   - Show the Three.js balance visualization
   - Explain: "Purple cubes = variables, Gold spheres = numbers"
   - Demonstrate how adding/removing from one side affects balance
   - Rotate camera to see different angles

3. **Learning Path Overview** (4 min)
   - Show the 5 worlds on menu screen
   - Explain progression: "You'll learn ONE skill at a time"
   - Show mastery requirement: "4 out of 5 correct to advance"
   - Demonstrate hint button and "Show Work" feature
   - Show "Copy to Gemini" feature for extra help

**Teacher Notes**:
- Model thinking process explicitly
- Show it's okay to use hints
- Emphasize mastery over speed
- Explain that mistakes help us learn

---

### üë• **Part 3: Guided Practice** (20 minutes)

**Objective**: Students practice with teacher support

**Activities**:

1. **Pair Work Setup** (2 min)
   - Pair students (mix abilities)
   - Each pair shares one device
   - Roles: Driver (controls game) & Navigator (guides thinking)
   - Switch roles every 2-3 problems

2. **Levels 1-3 Practice** (15 min)
   - Pairs work through Castle of Basics levels
   - Teacher circulates, asking:
     - "What's your first step?"
     - "How do you know you're right?"
     - "Can you explain this to your partner?"
   - Provide support to struggling pairs
   - Challenge advanced pairs: "Can you solve it in your head first?"

3. **Class Check-In** (3 min)
   - Ask: "Who completed Level 1? Level 2? Level 3?"
   - Quick discussion: "What strategy worked best?"
   - Address common mistakes: "I noticed some pairs forgot to..."

**Differentiation**:
- **Struggling**: Use Gemini helper, watch video tutorials
- **On-Level**: Continue Story Mode progression
- **Advanced**: Try Practice Arena - Complex Equations

**Teacher Notes**:
- Don't rescue too quickly - productive struggle is good
- Highlight great explanations between partners
- Keep energy high with encouragement

---

### üéÆ **Part 4: Independent Practice** (20 minutes)

**Objective**: Students work independently or continue pairs

**Activities**:

**Option A: Continue Story Mode** (Students choose)
- Work through Levels 4-6 (Distributive Property)
- Goal: Reach Level 7 or earn 500 XP
- Individual or continued pairs

**Option B: Creative Project** (Choice board)
Students can:
1. **Strategy Guide**: Document solving strategies for 5 different equation types
2. **Visual Explanation**: Screenshot 3D visualization and annotate
3. **Peer Challenge**: Create 10 custom equations for classmates
4. **Tutorial Creation**: Write step-by-step guide for one concept

**Teacher Role**:
- Monitor progress on screens
- Pull small groups for reteaching if needed
- Conference with students about their strategies
- Take screenshots of great work for sharing

**Assessment During This Time**:
- Check world progress percentages
- Note streak counters (consistency indicator)
- Observe XP earnings (effort indicator)
- Listen to student explanations

**Teacher Notes**:
- Some students will advance faster - that's okay!
- Projects allow for creativity and different learning styles
- Keep track of who needs more support

---

### üéØ **Part 5: Challenge & Assessment** (10 minutes)

**Objective**: Check for understanding and preview next concepts

**Activities**:

1. **Challenge Problems** (5 min)
   - Put 3 equations on board (mixed difficulty):
     1. `2x + 5 = 15` (basic)
     2. `3(x + 2) = 21` (distributive)
     3. `4x - 3 = 2x + 7` (both sides)
   - Students solve on paper/whiteboard
   - Share solutions
   - Vote on which was hardest

2. **Exit Ticket** (3 min)
   - **Quick Write**: "Explain in your own words how to solve an equation with variables on both sides"
   - OR **Quick Problem**: One equation to solve independently
   - OR **Reflection**: "What did you learn today? What's still confusing?"

3. **Preview & Homework** (2 min)
   - Show what's coming: "Next class we'll tackle the Mountain of Both Sides!"
   - Homework options:
     - Complete 3 more levels in Story Mode
     - Finish any creative project
     - Practice Arena (any mode) for 15 minutes
     - Watch remaining tutorial videos

**Teacher Notes**:
- Exit ticket informs next lesson planning
- Celebrate progress made today!
- Build excitement for next session

## üîÑ Modifications & Accommodations

### For ELL Students
- Use Gemini AI for translations
- Visual 3D balance heavily relied upon
- Pair with strong English speaker
- Allow native language notes

### For Students with IEPs
- Extended time (let them continue tomorrow)
- Fewer mastery requirements (3/5 instead of 4/5)
- Calculator allowed
- Reduced number of problems
- Text-to-speech for word problems

### For Gifted Students
- Skip to advanced levels
- Create complex custom equations
- Help create tutorial videos
- Challenge mode problems

---

## üí° Troubleshooting Tips

### Students Moving Too Fast
- Require written work alongside game
- Add project requirement
- Peer tutoring role

### Students Moving Too Slow
- Allow more hint usage
- Extended pair work time
- Focus on Levels 1-3 only today
- Tutorial video re-watch

### Technical Issues
- Have backup worksheets ready
- Pair students if device shortage
- Use projector for whole-class play
- Screenshot key screens beforehand
